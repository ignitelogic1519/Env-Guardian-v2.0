How it works

Four steps, fully automatic

From enrollment to enforcement to exit — the device does the work. Here's the real flow, with actual app screens.

1
Enroll once

Seal the device to its owner

The employee installs the app, grants permissions and registers. The device is sealed and permanently bound to their identity — a stolen or swapped phone can't be re-registered under someone else.

  • ✔ Captures device model + Android version
  • ✔ Anti-theft: one device, one owner
  • ✔ Background monitor starts automatically
2
Enter the zone

The app comes to you

Cross the geofence and a full-screen prompt brings Env Guardian forward: “Restricted Zone Entered — scan the QR to authenticate.” No need to hunt for the app; enforcement is already engaging.

  • ✔ Instant geofence detection
  • ✔ Auto-foreground prompt
  • ✔ Optional pre-scan gate to close background apps
3
Authenticate & enforce

Scan in — lockdown on

Scanning the zone QR verifies presence and starts the time-in-zone timer. From that moment, only whitelisted apps run, unapproved apps lose their network, per-app limits apply, and every decision is logged.

  • ✔ Presence-verified via QR (static or rotating)
  • ✔ Zero-trust app blocking + network guard
  • ✔ Live time-in-zone timer

Only approved apps

The Armory shows exactly what's allowed vs blocked in-zone.

Every decision logged

A live perimeter log records each allow/block with a timestamp.

4
Leave & restore

Out of the zone, back to normal

Cross the boundary and everything is released instantly — apps unblocked, network restored, timer reset. The phone is fully the employee's again, while the visit's compliance is reported to your server.

  • ✔ Automatic full-access restore
  • ✔ Compliance & activity reported
  • ✔ Nothing personal retained
🛠️
What does the admin do? Almost nothing day-to-day.

Admins define the zone, whitelist and time limits once (from a simple dashboard or the database). After that, devices enforce themselves and stream status back in real time. Need to freeze a device? One toggle. That's it.
